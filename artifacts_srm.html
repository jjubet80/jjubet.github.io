Discussion Topic: Discuss why Cyber Security is now a global issue and why it is important for companies to invest in Cyber Security

Initial Post

The evolving technology in the financial sector is now commonly known as FINTECH (Financial Technology). There is also, the traditional banking systems learning to accommodate financial technology hence, modifying its services to suit financial technology with new products as internet banking, ATM and digital currencies all relying on inter-connectivity (Dapp, T., Slomka, L., AG, D.B. and Hoffmann, R., 2015). The financial technology sector is evolving faster than the conventional banking as it incorporates the internet of things, connecting the telecommunication networks, computing and internet. There is also a growing interaction between the new financial technology companies and the existing traditional banks in terms of data exchange. These data exchanges amidst differing technological adaptations often lead to inconsistencies. These inconsistencies could also expose both the fintech institutions and the traditional banks to significant privacy issues, data breaches and cyber attacks (Najaf, K., Mostafiz, M.I. and Najaf, R., 2021). In 2016, the cyber security experts of Nigeria CSEAN, estimated that Nigeria for example, loses 127 billion naira annually to cyber attack. with the emergence of financial technology and digital businesses the figure is bound to triple (Faya, M. and Ogbuefi, N., 2019)

Financial technology has disrupted the banking process with an attendant increased in Cyber crime. Financial information is of high value target for many cyber criminals and Fintech firms have increasingly become attractive targets and typically, have fewer resources dedicated to cyber security. (Faya .M. And Ogbuefi .N.). In India, about 4800 computers were reported to be attacked by the wannacry ransomware which revealed that the attack was possible based on a weak Windows XP and unpatched Windows operating systems used by about 70% of the country's ATMs operating systems. It also stated that data of about 3.2 million debit cards was lost in what is claimed to the India's biggest breaches. SBI, HDFC Bank, ICICI, YES Bank and Axis were all hit by the breach of debit cards. Cyber attacks cost Indian businesses about $4 Billion every year as per latest estimates(Kumar, A., 2021). Furthermore, With the emergence of covid-19 pandemic and social distancing protocols, where people tend to live in isolation, there is an increased dependence on digital financial services (Alber, N. and Dabour, M., 2020).

India launched an Interface for Money mobile application (BHIM), which was downloaded 17 Million times within two months of launch. Also, PayTM, India's leading mobile payments service crossed the 200 Million users mark earlier this year, and have most recently launched PayTM bank with about $1.4 Billion raised from Softbank valuing the firm at $7 Billion (Kumar, A., 2021). These huge investments in FINTECH, needs to be securely guarded as cyber attacks on these platforms can be catastrophic. Regulators would also have to enact policies and regulation to ensure adequate level of cyber security and data privacy (Faya .M. And Ogbuefi .N.). Regulatory authorities have to respond quickly to the requirements for the stability of the financial system in the light of the of growth of digital payment sector, especially the ones resulting from FinTech (Alber, N. and Dabour, M., 2020)

The rapid growth in financial technology presents a risk of cyber attacks, security and privacy breaches hence the need to protect customer data and privacy in a similar matching approach. The cybersecurity market is expected to grow from USD 152.71 billion in 2018 to USD248.26 billion by 2023, at a Compound Annual Growth Rate of 10.2% during 2018–2023 (Adeyoju, A., 2019). Cyber security is therefore concluded a necessity in the present world global economy and therefore deserves the appropriate funding and attention to avert a global economic crisis in the financial industry.

Discussion Topic: Identify and discuss two security technologies and the context in which they can be employed. Your discussion could consider either network or/and software security issues. For example, if a network issue, what type of firewall would be ideal for the solution? What are the strengths and weaknesses of the solution?

Initial Post

Network security will presumably become more complex even as more organizations expand their internal networks and others are joining the internet. There are over 5,168,780,607 internet users in the world as of 31st March 2021 as against 16 million in 1995 (Internet World Stats, 2019). The complexity in network security can also be exacerbated by the rise in cybercrime in recent years. Controlling network access and placing restrictions can prevent security issues.

The Network Access Control (NAC) is a useful security tool as networks expand both internally and externally. This method of security is designed to give access to specified resources, and a defined set of users or devices. Access control identifies confidentiality, and then defines its availability, by granting access only to authorized users/networks, thereby ensuring network information integrity. A NAC system can deny network access to noncompliant devices, place them in a quarantined area, or give them only restricted access to computing resources, thus keeping insecure nodes from infecting the network (Saleh, M.F., 2011). The Cisco Identity Services Engine (ISE) which manages Cisco’s NAC, also extends trust and network visibility to the cloud technology (Cisco, N.D). While NAC can be employed as a security solution over the network, this has a limitation because it only focuses on controlling access to networks. it is effective for protecting against threats that are external to a network. It does not detect breaches after they occur or protect against “insider” threats that originate on an already-authenticated device (Order, 2021). Other security solutions will therefore be required along side this method for a more reliable network protection

Another network security solution that can be relied upon for internal security, and an extension to NAC is the stateful inspection firewall. A stateful inspection firewall verifies and keeps track of established connections, performs data packet inspection. It maintains a state table with source IP, destination IP, source port and destination port for a securely established connection. Stateful inspection firewalls works with the TCP handshake checking for legitimate connections as well as source and destination IPs to determine which data packets can pass through (Bonuccelli, 2020). The Linux iptables firewall technology is an open-source firewall which maintains iptables configured to define if a traffic is legitimate to flow from source to destination. (Mihalos, et al 2019). Although these extra checks provide advanced security, they consume a lot of system resources and can slow down traffic considerably. Hence, they are prone to DDoS (distributed denial-of-service attacks) (Bonuccelli, 2020). A splay tree filter or skip list can however be incorporated to classify and prioritize inspection packets, thereby accelerating the processing time (Khezrian & Abbasi, 2019).

A combination of different security technologies may be used for a more secured system. Security issues keep changing landscapes as cyber criminals’ device new means of attacking systems. A manipulation can occur that might render the current security systems ineffective hence, from another perspective, while securing the network is very important, emphasis can also be made on system resilience in the event of unknown form of attack. The challenges to Network security are enormous and perhaps the perspective of securing the network should also have a component of offence to deter cybercrime since the repercussions of attacking an offensive network could have devastating effect on the cybercriminal.

Peer response to Abidemi Adelakun
Hi Abidemi, A commendable feature of the Network Intrusion Detection System (NIDS) is its passive state which can work without interfering with the network traffic which they monitor
The NIDS in this state sniffs the internal interface of the firewall in read-only mode and sends alerts to a management server or an administrator via a different network interface.
Although this may come as a disadvantage as real threats can be missed, (False Negatives) since it does not strip off data packets to inspect potential threats in the packets, this feature of packet sniffing comes in handy in monitoring compliance and logging of events.
These events can be used to analyse security systems or implement more effective controls. The feature also generates false positives, but this can be fine-tuned based the organisations appetite

As you rightly mentioned, the Human factor is a prominent factor in cyber security and this is also one major reason why Insider threats are difficult to curtail
User behavioral analysis(UBA) will be a great tool for insider threats since the staff behaviors can be monitored to establish what is considered normal behavior or abnormal behavior This analysis may be based of a certain class/set of users or based on a user daily routine. It uses machine learning techniques to weigh the factors and, if the deviation looks significant, issues an alert. If a user is assigned new responsibilities, the system needs to take them into account and establish a new baseline
Although behavioral analysis might indicate suspicious activities among users, there is also the concern that these suspicious activities might not be properly interpreted without a sound understanding of the human psychology. Sometimes, people can do unusual things for legitimate reasons (Miller, 2020). Employee risk profiles can differ and can be complex. Certain negative emotions, stressors, and conflicts have long been associated with incidents of workplace aggression, employee turnover, absenteeism, accidents, fraud, sabotage, and espionage (Stroz et al.,2016). User Behavioral analysis is therefore quite complex and may require a factor of the human psychology incorporated into its security Architecture or framework.

Peer response to Bee Teck Ivan Chua
Thank you, Bee.
This is an interesting piece and quite practical. As you mentioned, the water treatment plant has two networks separated by a physical firewall. My attention was drawn to the advantage of this physical firewall and its implication to this security arrangement. The physical firewall is a hardware standalone device having its own resources, CPU, RAM, Disk space and operating software making it a robust solution for large networks. It doesn’t have to depend on shared resources like the software firewall (Konikiewicz and Markowski, 2017).
This arrangement shows that the operation network has no direct interface with the outside network and is again, protected by another physical firewall which separates it from the office network. The operation network which requires more protection, is hidden from the outside network therefore, the attack surface has been minimized. Insider threats have also been minimized by access granted to designated ports or url.
As you mentioned, the cost is higher and the setup and configuration are more complicated, however, the benefit will far outweigh this disadvantage in the long run considering the implication of a cyber-attack like ransomware attack, which can run into billions (Stu Sjouwerman, July 29, 2021). Also worth considering, is that some physical firewalls that come with proprietary operating systems, offer a great security advantage over general operating systems which might have security vulnerabilities that can be exploited by hackers (Upadhyay, 2020)
Interestingly also, is the interoperability for the physical firewall allowing it to run a VPN connection. This brings in other security forms to the network and enables the provision of access to authorized users even when they are not physically present and in this scenario, it was also used as a remote troubleshooting tool.
However, I am a bit worried about the effectiveness of this security setup against DDoS attack since a successful attack on the Office network means there may be ineffective monitoring of the PLC system. What do you think Bee?
