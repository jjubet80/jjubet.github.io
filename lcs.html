Modules learning objectives

  • Identify and explain the architecture, structure and functionality of basic components of a computer system, considering Cyber Security issues.
  • Critically evaluate implications of the key vulnerabilities and threats of software and network security and approaches to mitigate these issues.
  • Appraise critically and apply the concepts and principles of secure object oriented design to facilitate business security-based decisions and implementations.
  • Evaluate critically the solutions developed to solve/mitigate these security issues.

This is a multinational company with several branches across the globe that is connected via the internet. The implication of a data breach across any of their networks will result in consequences like breach of the Data protection policies, reputational damage, and loss of income. In my opinion, the company should invest more in cyber security because this is not only about protecting their assets, but also about compliance to regulations The seminar questions that needed to be answered were: • Investigate the various security concerns this company has had, and might have in the future • Provide exemplars of how these concerns have been or can be dealt with by the company. Have you reviewed potential roles to take up during/after this course? If so, why have you chosen a particular role? These questions were answered during the seminar and it was identified that the company will have more security challenges in terms of scale because of its large coverage (found in more than 70 countries) and digitisation technologies. The company could be exposed to malware attacks, insider attacks and privacy issues because of legacy systems, delay in updates and patches, database systems management. The solutions to these issues lies in the CIA triangle(Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability). By doing so, they need to abide by different country jurisditions laws and rules and applying it to IT infrastructure, conform to GDPR rules, invest in good Cyber security Technology, train their staff on security breaches, regular data monitoring, embrace cloud technologies. The security solutions should be across, network security, software security and human factors. The company will require specialist in most field of cyber security and If I were to work with one, I will prefer the security Architect. I like to find solutions to issues. Unit 3: Approaches to Cyber Security design

This unit covered the approaches to cyber security design. A lecture case was presented which discussed the psychology of cyber security where attackers use the human psychology with attacks such as pretexting, phishing etc. lecture discussed the UK national cyber security center principles for secured system design and focused on secured approaches to Network and software design. The lecture noted thread identification methods like STRIDE, Abuse cases, Attack trees and protection, OSWAP top 10 web application security risk and their secured security measures. The lessons presented more professional details in the aspect cyber security exposing us to the idea of identifying various forms of security threats and finding solutions to these threats especially, understanding that threats are also evolving. In my Opinion, this unit is a very important unit in the sense that it is an introduction to the core cyber security issues and understanding this aspect will determine the way I will understand this course as a whole. So far, the concept is quite clear and I understand that cyber security design approach is Earn or give, but never assume trust, Use an authentication mechanism that cannot be compromised, Authorise after you authenticate, Ensure all data are explicitly validated, Use cryptography correctly, Identify sensitive data and mechanism to handle, Users must always be considered, Understand the effects of changes to integrating external components and attack surface, Flexibility is required when changing objects and actors and Separating data and control instructions; never process control instructions received from untrusted sources.

Unit 4: Threat identification and modelling

This Unit focused on an example of web-based information system where potential threats were identified using threat modelling techniques. Questions below were asked; • Review 3 threat modelling techniques that you have found are more commonly used in any industry of your choice • In deciding what techniques to apply, what considerations need to be made to professional, legal, social and ethical concerns? A seminar was presented on Picture Archiving Communications System (PACS) information system to address these questions The PACS system is a method of accessing medical information electronically in picture form and storing this data. The system mimics the manual file systems in hospitals for the radiology departments. In securing such a system, the CIA triangle will be considered and tips to adopt is ensuring no blind spots in the design, penetration must be difficult with minimal attack surfaces, system resilience should be considered, have ability to detect suspicious or attack activities in real time The Abuse case threat modelling identified 2 major actors. The Malicious staff who may want to delete patient data, or a cyber criminal who may want to use the brute force to acquire admin password. The attack tree threat modelling identifies the ultimate goal of the cyber criminal and ways to achieve this goal in form of a tree. Protection trees are also drafted in the same pattern identifying measure to protect against such threats. The STRIDE method is identifying spoofing, Tampering, Repudiation, Information disclosure, Denial of service and Elevation of privilege With threats aiming at Authentication, changing content of database, clearing activity logs, access to vital or restricted information, disrupting system availability and authorisation. The threats should be assessed to determine their impact either high, medium or low and identify matching security approach These Topics have exposed me to the different attack patterns and models use to analyse them. The concept of STRIDE and attack trees is however more appealing to me. STRIDE gives me an impression of a simple but yet complete approach. Implementation becomes easier once these threats have been identified. The Attack tree also gives an impression of a sequential and hierarchical approach and like working through a mess of cybercrime incidences

Unit 7: Database Design and Implementation

The unit started with a Lecture cast on Database systems. One of the key components of information management system was object of focus in this unit. The database is a very critical part of any application. Its design and implementation with focus on security implications were exhaustively discussed. Concepts and principles of database design was thoroughly explained. Data management and the role that data plays in any and every organization was highlighted. Tracking business activities and generating information which can be analysed for insightful action for improve business profitability was discussed as some of the uses of database in modern business operations. Database, Database system, Database management system (DBMS), data entities, attributes and keys were all explained. DBMS is a software used to build, manage, and manipulate a database. The two approaches to data management are traditional approach and database approach and were extensively discussed. Data redundancy and data integrity are the major challenges associated with the traditional approach although it might tend to work very well with smaller task. Data maintenance might be very difficult to achieve. The alternative is the database approach with several benefits such as controlled data redundancy which makes maintenance easy and increases flexibility of the data. It makes data sharing easy but requires a database management system to be able to effectively implement it. Three database models were considered, and they include the Hierarchical model, Network model and Relational model. The relational model is the most intuitive to implement and hence its wide usage in application. The major database design considerations that were discussed are type of content, nature of access to be granted, logical structure of the database as well as the physical organization. Entity Relationship Diagrams (ERD) and their uses were properly explained. Examples were provided to aid my understanding. The Entity-Relationship Diagrams (ERD) deals with the logical aspect of a database design as an abstract model of how a database is structured to meet requirements. It identifies the relationship between data items. A database manipulation programming language called Structural Query Language (SQL) which was developed in 1970s was introduced as programming language for standardizing database operations and manipulations. Database security as a major part of cyber security was highlighted and this involves preserving database confidentiality, integrity, and availability and database protection tools major functionalities must include discovery, data activity monitoring, encryption and tokenisation, data security optimization and risk analysis. The unit concluded by introducing emerging trends in databases which include distributed databases, object-oriented databases, image database, Hypertext database, Hypermedia database, Graph database The collaborative discussion forum 2 continued in this unit. Unit 9: OOP Application of Principles and Concepts

This unit focussed on four key areas that will enhance security of applications. They include access control and privileges, integrating MySQL and Python in application, Database management with Python, and secured password and authentication implementation using Python scripts. The access control and account management features discussed include the following: it allows users the opportunity to connect to database and again access to restricted content through provision of their username and passwords which has been registered in the database beforehand. The host system authenticates users and the systems they are using to gain access and assigns the corresponding privileges associated with the account. The process is concluded when restricted contents are made available to the user based on privilege level set on their user account in the database. There is no connection whatsoever between operating system user account and database or application user account. The user accounts in the database are stored in the users table of the database while user passwords are encrypted with an encryption method specified during database design. MySQL comes with an initial Database Administrator account called root. They are two main categories of privileges associated with MySQL database and it includes administrative privileges which are global and applies to the entire server while the database privileges are specific to a particular database and all objects within the database. A detailed guidelines for granting privileges was extensively discussed and its importance highlighted. Correct syntax for implementation of basic task like creating user account, how to specify account names, wildcards creation and management using different Network IP address classes were discussed and demonstrated. Further demonstrations were done using the root account in MySQL database. Three encrypted connections were identified and discussed which include server side, client side and mandatory encryption connections, their benefits and use cases were also highlighted. Their correct syntax for their implementations were properly explained and demonstrated.

Unit 10: Secure Web development

This unit was dedicated to understanding the key concepts and principles of web applications development as modern businesses cannot effectively function without them. It further explored the technologies and frameworks for web application development. Considering merits, demerits, use cases and best practices. The main components of any web application are hardware, the operating system, a scripting language, a database, and a Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP) server. They form the building blocks upon which any other activity will depend upon. The Operating System (OS) is responsible for resource allocations and management to the application. It also allows communication between hardware and software and allow users to interreact with the application and hardware. There are a lot of Operating Systems available today and they include Windows, Linux, Mac, Unix, Sun Solaris and many more. The HTTP Server, which is a software, used in web application, compiles the results from Scripting Language, Database and Hypertext Markup Language commonly known as HTML file to generate content for users. HTTP Server enables sharing of webpages, scripts and files between developers and end users. It is also responsible for tracking files that belong to server or end user’s computer. The HTTP server has the responsibility of presenting outputs from databases and scripting languages into HTML for browsers to display on end user’s computer. They are different types of browsers with varying features for the end users to view their HTML content. They include Google chrome, Microsoft Edge, Mozilla Firefox, Opera, and many others. Some examples of HTTP server are Apache and Microsoft Internet Information Services (IIS). The Scripting language is divided into two parts, the Client-side and Server-side scripting languages. This software component of web development is used to express complex logical instructions and processes. The scripting programming language must support database and integration of internet protocols to be able to send data and messages over the network. The examples of scripting programming languages are Java, JavaScript, PHP, Python, Ruby, Perl, C#, C++ etc. Database is another crucial component of any and every web application. There are two technological options available for the implementation of databases. This is either by SQL or NoSQL. The most used examples of SQL-based implementation include MySQL, MariaDB, Cloud DB and many others while the most common NoSQL implementation is a document-oriented database called MongoDB. This is relatively new technology, and it is superefficient and very fast compared to the SQL-based technologies. The architecture of web application is another important part of the entire process that was discussed. The MVC architecture which has three parts was discussed and they include The Model, The View and the Controller.

Unit 11: Introduction to Secure Web Programming with DJango

This unit focuses on web application development using Django. It explores and implements some web application components using this framework. It explored technologies and libraries available to implement some of the security solutions discussed during the module. Django is a High level python based web development framework or software that makes it easy to build dynamic websites and supports the properties of common framework. Dynamic websites are sites whose contents are created based on each user requirement. Most frequent programming task have shortcuts on how to implement them using Django. It is very easy to retrieve records from a database or carry out a task based on user input by using Django framework. Mapping of requested URLs to code that handles requests to specify username and other associated parameters are normally used. HTML forms can easily be displayed, validated and redisplayed with ease by the use of this framework. Data type manipulation through data submitted by user is extremely ease to implement by the use of Django. It works with template system where content is separated from presentation and can easily integrate with storage layers to offer versatility to its operations. There are other popular frameworks for web developments and they include Smarty, Yii framework, Zend framework. Django encourages loose coupling approach in its implementation and emphasis that applications should communicate to each other by the use of Application Programming Interfaces (APIs). Its main properties are Template, Database access system, HTTP request/response layer, Caching and also has support for the Model View Controller (MVC) architecture. Where Model defines Database and update data items, View defines user interface and Controller defines business logic. The major issues with traditional frameworks were highlighted and discussed and the need to embrace Django explained. Some of the issues are where to keep your code and the security implication of the decision. Keeping your code somewhere that is personal to you provides additional layer of security unlike in traditional framework where codes are kept in the root directory which is known to all. Python 2.3 or higher version is requirement for Django to operate unhindered.

Unit 12: A Look into the Future of Cyber Security

This unit examines in details all the various topics covered in the module and tries to take a gaze at the future of Cyber Security. It explored Cyber Security areas which provide you with some perspective about the various career options that are available in this field of Cyber Security. Trends and specialised knowledge that are required of Cyber Security professional were also pointed out in this unit. The impact of emerging technologies on Cyber Security were extensively addressed while privacy and data confidentiality implications on these emerging technologies were also examined. The first emerging technology discussed is The Internet of Things (IoT) and this is revolutionising almost every aspect of the society and, by extension, industries. As the fifth-generation (5G) networks become more readily accessible across many countries and continents of the world, the growth is going to be exponential as more devices and services get connected, there are over 50 billion devices that have connected and many more are connecting on daily basis. The Internet of Everything (IoE) seeks to describe the fact that it is not only devices that are connected but, connection now includes people, processes, data and things and this goes to show that all things are now interconnected. A step further by the IoE is the Internet of Nano Things (loNT). Here, nano-sensors are being incorporated in diverse objects and through the use of nano-networks. The second emerging technology discussed is Cloud Computing and Manufacturing, The main idea behind Cloud computing is to provide on-demand computing services with high reliability, scalability and availability in a distributed environment.In Cloud computing, everything is treated as a service (EaaS, XaaS, *aaS), which may include Software as a Service (SaaS), Platform as a Service (PaaS) and Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) and many more. PayPal services was used as an excellent example to illustrate software as a service. Companies focus on other areas of their operation while utilizing PayPal to manage their payment services. Companies like Facebook, Google AppEngine, and Amazon EC2 are some of the common examples of PaaS. This allows users or businesses to use those platforms to carry out business activities without incurring any cost or knowing the internal workings of those systems. While Microsoft Azure is an excellent example of Infrastructure as a service (IaaS). You get all features of an enterprise server and many more in using Microsoft Azure. The payment for everything as a Service is normally pay as you Go (PAYG) Edge computing is the third emerging technology from the internet evolution that was discussed. It was designed to collect data from traditional database setups, sensor devices as well as mobile devices. Unlike cloud computing (where the data is processed by a cloud service), edge computing allows processing to be carried out at the network edge so that processing (including the devices and where the data is collected) is secure. There are several places that Edge computing can be applied due to its speed of execution and security. It can be used in finding a missing child by deploying data to the edge devices for the search to be done locally. Security threats have increased due to the fact that devices have become part of the Internet of Things which enables the storage, analysis, monitoring, and sharing of vast quantities of data with other networked devices and users. Evolution has added another security risks that device manufacturers and application developers never anticipated and therefore there is an urgent need for cyber security experts to up their game to adequately respond to these new security challenges. This is because many devices now have to be configured to function in an environment where they were not originally designed to be used and the privacy of users’ is threatened because of their limited control and choice over the collection, retention, and distribution of their data.’ Despite the fact that there are a lot of security tools out there, the demand for smart security tools and methods will increase to be able to keep up with the security threats discussed as result of the technological evolution. Artificial Intelligence (AI) will be a handy tool in responding to the security challenges been discovered through evolution of technology. By training a cyber-security bot with deep learning, an AI could be made that scans for and predicts or detects threats nearly perfectly and hence offering a better protection. Finally, it is important to develop positive security culture in organizations, and this area of research focuses on security risk protocols that can be applied across various settings and jurisdictions. To achieve an excellent and effective security solution, all parties and participants must be involved and play the game by all stipulated rules of engagement.
